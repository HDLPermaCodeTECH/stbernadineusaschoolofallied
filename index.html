Welcome to St. Bernadine School of Allied Health, Healthcare Services

Compassion, Competence, and Commitment - Top Rated in Hudson County

St. Bernadine School of Allied Health, Healthcare Services is a nurse-led institution providing premier professional clinical services, global career procurement, and specialized clinical instruction since 1986. The backbone of our services is a spirit of excellence, clinical competence, and commitment matched with a modern set of advanced medical proficiencies.

Paramount to our services is using a single point of connection to our pursuit of optimizing the well-being of our home care clients, assuring our Healthcare Professionals and students flourish in their future careers, and supporting our industry partners to prosper in their business.

We believe in a professional approach to healthcare, and our team is here to support you every step of the way.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our programs and admissions process.

What are the admission requirements for the CNA program?

Applicants must be at least 18 years old, have a high school diploma or GED, and pass a background check and TB test.

Do you offer financial aid or payment plans?

Yes! We offer flexible payment plans and can assist you in exploring various financial aid options for those who qualify.

How long does the CNA certification take?

Our standard full-time CNA program takes 6 weeks to complete, including classroom instruction and clinical rotations.
